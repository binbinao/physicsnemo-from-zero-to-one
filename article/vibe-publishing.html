🔑 5 Non-Obvious Learnings

1. Plans > Prompts. A 300-word detailed writing plan produces better output than a 30-word prompt every single time. The bottleneck is never "AI can't write" — it's "you haven't thought clearly enough about what you want."
2. Parallel execution changes everything. Writing Ch3, Ch4, and Ch5 simultaneously? Impossible for a human. Trivial for agents. This is where the 30× comes from — concurrency, not speed.
3. The Review Agent is the hero. First drafts are 70% good. The review agent catches the other 30% — wrong function names, deprecated APIs, logic gaps. Without it, you ship garbage.
4. Human taste is the moat. Agents don't know what makes a great tutorial vs. a mediocre one. When to use a joke, when a diagram beats 500 words, when "start over" is the right call — that's the human job. And it's the hardest job.
5. This isn't laziness — it's a role upgrade. From "solo writer" to "editor-in-chief + architect." You design the experience. Agents do the heavy lifting. You quality-control the output.

🛠️ If You Want to Try This Yourself

• Start with a plan, not a prompt
